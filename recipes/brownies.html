Brownies

Description

Claire Saffitz named these her “forever” brownies because it took so much work to get them right that she’s vowed to never develop another brownie recipe again.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup Dutch process cocoa powder (0.7 oz / 20g)
  • 5 ounces (142g) semisweet chocolate (preferably 64 to 68% cacao), coarsely chopped
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter (3 oz / 85g), cut into pieces
  • ¼ cup neutral oil, such as vegetable or grapeseed (2 oz / 56g)
  • ½ cup granulated sugar (3.5 oz / 100g)
  • ½ cup packed dark brown sugar (3.5 oz / 100g)
  • 1 large egg (1.8 oz / 50g)
  • 2 large egg yolks (1.1 oz / 32g)
  • 1½ teaspoons vanilla extract
  • ¾ cup all-purpose flour (3.5 oz / 100g)
  • 2 tablespoons malted milk powder (0.63 oz / 18g) (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt (0.11 oz / 3g)
  • 6 ounces (170g) milk chocolate, coarsely chopped (1 cup)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F and prepare line an 8 × 8-inch pan with 2 sheets of foil. Lightly butter the foil and set aside.
  2. In a large heatproof bowl, whisk the cocoa powder and ¼ cup boiling water (4 oz / 113g) until smooth (this will bring out the flavor of the cocoa).
  3. Melt the chocolate, butter, and oil over a medium saucepan filled with about 1 inch of simmering water. Warm the mixture gently, stirring occasionally, until the chocolate and butter are melted and the mixture is smooth.
  4. Whisk the granulated and brown sugars into the chocolate mixture. Add the whole egg, egg yolks, and vanilla and whisk vigorously until the mixture looks very thick, smooth, and glossy.
  5. Add the flour, malted milk powder, and salt and whisk slowly until everything is combined, then whisk more vigorously until the batter is very thick, a full 45 seconds.
  6. Add the milk chocolate to the batter and fold with a flexible spatula to distribute. Scrape the batter into the prepared pan, spreading in an even layer all the way to the corners.
  7. Bake the brownies until the surface is shiny and puffed and the center is dry to the touch but still soft when pressed, 25 to 30 minutes.
  8. Allow the brownies to cool in the pan until they are no longer hot, about 1 hour, then refrigerate until the bottom of the pan feels cold, about 1 hour longer. Enjoy!
